```md
# PRD: Post-call Risk Brief

## Human-readable summary

Ship a lightweight workflow that converts a customer call transcript into a short brief a CSM can review, edit, and use for follow-up. The pilot is optimized for time saved and workflow usefulness, not for a perfect churn prediction model.

## User and job

- Primary user: mid-market SaaS customer success manager
- Job to be done: quickly understand what happened on a call, what risk signals appeared, and what follow-up should happen next

## In scope

- transcript ingest
- 5-bullet call summary
- 2-3 evidence-linked risk cues
- follow-up recommendations
- editable brief output

## Out of scope

- CRM writeback
- portfolio-level health scoring
- automated playbooks

## Dependencies

- transcript source available within 15 minutes of call end
- prompt or model stack that supports structured output
- lightweight review UI or export surface

## Constraints

- no system-of-record integration in v1
- 6-week pilot window
- team capacity: 2 engineers, 1 designer

## Success metrics

- at least 5 pilot CSMs use the brief on 3 or more calls per week
- at least 60% report time saved on follow-up prep
- fewer than 10% of briefs require complete discard due to bad output

## Fallback and escalation

- if transcript quality is low, return summary-only mode and suppress risk labeling
- if the model cannot support evidence-linked risk cues, require manual confirmation before the brief is marked complete

## Agent-readable task spec

### Inputs

- raw transcript
- account name
- call date

### Required outputs

- summary bullets
- risk cue list with transcript evidence
- recommended follow-ups
- confidence note if evidence is weak

### Acceptance checks

- every risk cue must cite transcript evidence
- no invented product facts
- follow-up recommendations must map to something said on the call

## Review checklist

- are risk cues evidence-linked
- is the brief short enough for immediate use
- does the output suppress certainty when evidence is weak
- are out-of-scope integrations avoided
```
