# 11 Must-Install Commands

These are the fastest way to understand the system.

## 1. `pm-command-center`

Use when you are not sure which job you are really doing yet. It routes you to the shortest useful workflow.

## 2. `find-winning-direction`

Use when the starting point is a rough founder or PM idea, an existing product to audit, or a pile of strategy context and you need a wedge, business model, channel, and strategic narrative worth pursuing.

## 3. `shape-idea`

Use when the starting point is rough, underspecified, or still mostly instinct.

## 4. `validate-demand`

Use when you have notes, comments, tickets, interviews, or URLs and need to know whether demand is real.

## 5. `agent-prd`

Use when the direction is good enough to specify and the output must work for humans and agents.

## 6. `prioritize`

Use when competing initiatives all sound important and someone needs to say what to do now, later, or never.

## 7. `define-metrics`

Use when a feature or workflow needs a success definition, a guardrail, and a measurement window.

## 8. `design-experiment`

Use when you have a hypothesis and need a real test instead of more internal debate.

## 9. `design-eval`

Use when you are shipping an AI feature or agent behavior and need pass/fail criteria plus fallback rules.

## 10. `run-roadmap`

Use when a chosen direction now needs milestones, ownership, delegation, and check-in rules.

## 11. `make-content`

Use when internal work should become external distribution without starting from a blank page.

## Why These First

They cover the highest-value 100X PM chain:

- direction selection
- business model framing
- channel choice
- idea shaping
- demand validation
- spec quality
- trade-offs
- metrics
- experiments
- AI behavior quality
- delegated execution
