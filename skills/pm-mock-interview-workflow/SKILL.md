## Purpose

Guide PMs through a full mock interview preparation workflow: clarify the target role, inventory the strongest stories, simulate likely interview questions, identify weak spots, and finish with a focused practice plan. Use this to move from vague confidence to interview-ready signal that matches the role you actually want.

This is not a list of sample questions. It is a workflow that connects role targeting, resume signal, story quality, and interview performance into one prep loop.

## Key Concepts

### What Strong PM Interview Prep Requires

Good interview prep is usually built from four layers:

1. **Target-role calibration**
   - What role are you interviewing for?
   - What does that company type care about?
   - What does "strong" look like at that level?

2. **Story inventory**
   - 5-7 stories that cover:
     - product sense
     - execution
     - strategy
     - cross-functional leadership
     - conflict / failure / trade-offs

3. **Question simulation**
   - behavioral
   - product design / sense
   - prioritization / analytics
   - role-specific depth

4. **Debrief and repetition**
   - where answers are thin
   - where evidence is weak
   - where the answer does not match the target role

### What This Workflow Optimizes For

- sharper answers, not perfect scripts
- defensible stories, not memorized theater
- role fit, not generic interview polish

### When to Use This

- Before first-round or panel PM interviews
- After resume rewriting, when you need the verbal story to match
- When interviews feel "fine" but not convincing

### When Not to Use This

- When you still do not know what role you want
- When the resume itself is clearly broken and needs work first
- When you need domain knowledge, not interview structure

## Application

This workflow runs in **5 phases**. Complete them in order.

## Phase 1: Clarify the Target Role

**Goal:** Define what this interview prep is for.

### Activities

1. Capture:
   - target title
   - company type or stage
   - interview stage
   - likely interview focus

2. Write a one-line calibration statement:
   - "This prep is for a [role] interview where I need to signal [top 3 things]."

### Output

- Target-role calibration note
- 3-5 likely evaluation dimensions

## Phase 2: Build the Story Inventory

**Goal:** Turn scattered experience into reusable stories.

### Activities

1. Identify 5-7 stories across:
   - product decision under ambiguity
   - measurable outcome
   - cross-functional conflict
   - trade-off or prioritization call
   - failure / miss / learning

2. For each story, capture:
   - context
   - your role
   - decision made
   - outcome
   - what it proves about you

### Output

- Story bank with coverage map
- Gaps where no strong story exists yet

## Phase 3: Run the Mock Question Set

**Goal:** Simulate the questions most likely to matter.

### Activities

1. Generate a short question set:
   - 3 behavioral
   - 2 product / strategy
   - 2 role-specific

2. Answer in full sentences, not notes.

3. For each answer, score:
   - clarity
   - evidence density
   - ownership
   - role fit

### Output

- Question set
- Draft answers
- Quick score per answer

## Phase 4: Debrief the Weak Spots

**Goal:** Identify where you are still not convincing.

### Activities

1. Diagnose weak spots:
   - answer too tactical
   - weak business impact
   - unclear ownership
   - not enough structure
   - wrong altitude for the role

2. Rewrite the weakest 2-3 answers.

3. Flag which weak spots are really resume problems versus interview problems.

### Output

- Interview weakness map
- Rewritten answers for weakest areas
- Recommendation to revisit related skills if needed

## Phase 5: Create the 7-Day Practice Plan

**Goal:** Turn feedback into a repeatable prep plan.

### Activities

1. Pick the top 3 themes to improve.
2. Assign practice reps for each theme.
3. Define a final mock run before the real interview.

### Output

- 7-day practice plan
- final checklist for interview day

## Examples

### Example Scenario

**Target:** Senior PM at a B2B SaaS company

**Weakness found in debrief:** The candidate has solid stories, but keeps talking about activity and process instead of the decision and the business result.

**Good workflow outcome:**
- sharper role calibration
- better story bank
- rewritten answers with stronger impact language
- 7-day plan focused on clarity, altitude, and measurable outcomes

### Example Question Types

- "Tell me about a time you had to choose between speed and quality."
- "How would you prioritize three requests from sales, support, and engineering?"
- "What metric would you use to know this product change worked?"

## Common Pitfalls

### Pitfall 1: Preparing Answers Without a Role Thesis

**Symptom:** Good stories, wrong emphasis.

**Fix:** Start with what the target role actually needs to believe about you.

### Pitfall 2: Memorizing Instead of Rehearsing

**Symptom:** Answers sound robotic and collapse under follow-up.

**Fix:** Learn the structure and proof points, not a script.

### Pitfall 3: Overusing Resume Language

**Symptom:** Interview answers sound like bullet points read aloud.

**Fix:** Expand the decision, trade-off, and context behind the bullet.

### Pitfall 4: Practicing Only Strength Stories

**Symptom:** Candidate is strong on wins and weak on conflict, failure, or trade-offs.

**Fix:** Include uncomfortable stories in the prep loop.
