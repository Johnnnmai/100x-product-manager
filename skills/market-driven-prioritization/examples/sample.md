# Example: Market-Driven Prioritization

## Scenario / 场景

A B2B SaaS company needs to prioritize features for Q2.

---

## Good Example / 好的例子

### Feature List

| Feature | Impact | Validation Status | Evidence | Priority |
|---------|--------|-------------------|----------|----------|
| API Integration | High | 🟢 Validated | 5 enterprise customers explicitly requested | P1 |
| Mobile App | High | 🔴 Not Validated | Only internal assumption | P3 |
| Dashboard Redesign | Medium | 🟡 Partial | Mixed feedback, 3 want it, 2 don't care | P2 |
| Dark Mode | Low | 🟡 Partial | Nice to have, no strong demand | P4 |

### Analysis

**P1 - DO FIRST:**
- API Integration has clear market demand
- Customers willing to pay extra for this

**P2 - SCHEDULE:**
- Dashboard Redesign needs more validation
- Schedule for Q3 after more user research

**P3 - VALIDATE FAST:**
- Mobile App could be big, but needs validation
- Run 5 customer interviews before Q3 planning

**P4 - DROP:**
- Dark Mode has low impact and low validation
- Deprioritize unless customers ask

---

## Bad Example / 错误的例子

### Feature List

| Feature | Impact | Validation Status | Evidence | Priority |
|---------|--------|-------------------|----------|----------|
| AI Features | High | 🔴 Not Validated | "AI is the future" | P1 |

### Problem

- No market validation
- Internal opinion ("AI is hot")
- Prioritizing based on trends, not market need

---

## Key Takeaway / 关键要点

**Always ask: "Does the market need this?"**

If you can't answer with evidence, validate first.
